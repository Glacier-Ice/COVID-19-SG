         CLUSTER SECURITY GUARDS CHERAS

         MOH has identified a cluster involving controllers
         and security personnel in a shopping center in Cheras,
         Kuala Lumpur. As of May 7, 2020 at 12 noon, a total of
         ten (10) COVID-19 positive cases have been reported in this cluster
         involving nine (9) and a citizen of Nepal
         Malaysian.


        The source of infection for the cluster is still under investigation. Step
        containment and preventive measures have been taken further
        such as screening tests to identify and carry out COVID-19
        for all employees and their close contacts.

        All cases in this cluster Nepalese living in
        the same residence in Cheras. Screening COVID-19 was
        carried out on their home residents, controller and
        security personnel at the shopping center, as well
        supermarket workers are closely related. So far, a total of 436 people
        were screened, of which 10 cases were positive, 347 negative cases and
        79 persons are still pending.

        District Health Office (DHO) Cheras and Titiwangsa will continue
        conduct monitoring on the increase in cases related COVID-19
        This cluster. All close contacts of each case was called for
        the purpose of screening and health education was also given.

        RELATED HEALTH ADVICE COVID-19

        The employers of various sectors of the economy that are allowed to operate
        must adhere to the terms and Standard Operating
        Procedure (SOP) established by the authorities by
        each sector.

        Furthermore, it is very important for employers and all
        workers to always follow the correct practices in the workplace
        respectively as:
           • Avoid being in crowded areas (crowded space);
           • Avoid being in an enclosed area (confined space)
             en masse at the same time, such as in space
             eating (pantry) at the workplace;
           • Maintain a distance of 1 meter when talking face to face with people
             other (close conversation).

        Ministry of Health will continue to monitor the incidence of this infection
        through information obtained from the Organization
        World Health Organization (WHO). People will always
        informed of any latest developments. so
