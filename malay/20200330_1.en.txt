        CAPACITY HOSPITAL SUPPLIES FOR SUPPORT
        PATIENTS COVID-19
        The Ministry of Health (MOH) has implemented
        preparedness measures in hospital services
        COVID-19 deal with the epidemic. Since February 2020, ie before
        enforcing government on 18th Movement Control Order
        March 2020, the Ministry of Health has identified a total of 57 hospitals
        screening and 26 hospitals that provide treatment COVID -19.
        At that time, the number of positive cases COVID-19 was 790
        cases compared to 2,626 cases in today.
        Now, with a variety of business, there was an increase of 70 hospitals
        providing screening services COVID-19 in all
        the country compared to 57 previously. Hospital care COVID-19
        increased from 26 to 38, through the cooperation of the Navy
        Forces and the university hospital of Medical Center
        University of Malaya. Seven (7) of the hospital is
        special hospital for the treatment of patients COVID -19.
        Thus, the number of beds for treatment COVID-19 increased
        to 3,994 beds. MOH is also planning
        increasing the number of beds through the identification and
        renovation of the Training Institute of the Ministry of Health (ILKKM) to be
        quarantine and treatment centers with a total of 1,937 beds
        fruit. ILKKM capacity can be improved if necessary.
        Other facilities were also identified, such as the Malaysian Agro
        Exposition Park Serdang (MAEPS) seeks to provide 600
        bed.


            RELATED HEALTH ADVICE COVID-19
            Taking into account the current situation, the Ministry of Health would like to advise people
            public to adopt health advisories that have been
            given by the Ministry of Health. All Malaysians can
            play a role in addressing the spread of infection
            COVID-19 in Malaysia.
            For individuals who have been ordered to undergo "home / self
            surveillance "be sure to follow the procedures of Order
            Monitoring and Observation in the house that has been
            mode. For the record, Command and Control
            The observations in this house is enshrined in the directive
            The Prevention and Control of Infectious Diseases, 1988 (Act
            342).
            Ministry of Health would like to advise individuals with a history
            travel abroad, you have to isolate yourself
            from associating closely with family members or people who
            living with you for 14 days, 24 hours a day.
            During this period, you are advised to take measures
            prevention such as good personal hygiene practices, often
            hand washing and social distancing (distance yourself at
            least one meter) of others.
            Within 14 days, you are also asked to monitor themselves
            any symptoms such as fever, cough, runny nose, sore throat or
            difficulty breathing. If you experience any symptoms
