      BRIEF EVALUATION OF THE SITUATION AFTER EXECUTION
      ORDER OF MOVEMENT CONTROL FOR TWO (2)
      WEEK
      After two weeks of implementation Movement Control Order
      (PKP), we can see the trend of the number of new cases reported
      per day (Appendix 2). It was found that the number of daily cases continue
      increased and sometimes horizontal, while the number of daily cases
      are discharged from the ward to increase every day. These data show
      the impact of the activities carried out by the Ministry of Health and various
      agency within one month late. However,
      The next two weeks are important, where it
      will determine whether the measures taken by the government
      so this impact is expected. this is absolutely
      closely linked to the behavior of people in compliance
      The Movement Control Order.

      Statistics also show that there are two (2) age groups
      the highest positive cases compared COVID-19
      other age groups. Two groups are in
      between 26 to 30 years and 56 to 60 years.





       RELATED HEALTH ADVICE COVID-19

       Motion Control Order (EFS) second phase will start
       with effect from 1 April 2020 until 14 April 2020, while
       some locations have been declared as a location for Control Order
       Bolted Movement (Enhanced Movement Control Order).
       As announced, the next two weeks are
       very important where cooperation of all parties is very
       necessary to ensure that the chain of infection can COVID-19
       disconnected.

       Thus, the Ministry of Health would like to advise people to sit
       house ( 'stay at home') while practicing ethical hygiene
       such as frequent hand washing and distance yourself (social
       distancing) to each other. Should have symptoms,
       use of masks. In addition, people also j
       advised to adopt the following items;
         • Eat healthy by providing healthy menu and
          balanced. Reduce your intake of sugar, salt and fat,
          while exaggerating consumption of fruits and vegetables
          vegetables.
         • Take an active lifestyle with physical activity
          or light exercise.
         • For those who smoke, use this opportunity to
          stop smoking. Non-smokers can also 'speak out'
          to family members who smoke to all free
          of the dangers of smoking and secondhand smoke.
         • Cultivate a positive mindset yourself and family members.
          Practice relaxation techniques are properly reflected in the mind
          in their daily life.
