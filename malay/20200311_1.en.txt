PRESS RELEASE
                   MINISTRY OF HEALTH MALAYSIA

          CURRENT SITUATION OF INFECTION DISEASE coronavirus 2019
                        (COVID-19) IN MALAYSIA
                            11 MAC 2020

        CURRENT STATUS CONFIRMED CASES COVID-19 HAS RECOVERED

        Ministry of Health (MOH) announced today that
        78th case of confirmed positive Coronavirus Disease 2019 (COVID-19)
        previously been recovered and allowed to discharge. This makes the 26 cases
        have fully recovered so far from COVID-19 and discharged
        of the ward.

        LATEST CASE STATUS COVID-19 IN MALAYSIA

        Recently, the Ministry of Health would like to inform that as of March 11, 2020, hours
        12 noon, there were 20 new cases, 19 were COVID
        reported. The contacts of this case is also close to being identified
        and close contact tracing activities are still being carried up
        the cause in which infection occurs can be identified.

        With the 20 new cases, bringing the cumulative total
        COVID-19 positive cases in Malaysia is of 149 cases.
        Of these:
           22 cases were diagnosed in Patient-Under-Investigation
             (PUI) infection COVID-19;
           124 cases among close contacts;
           one (1) in the case of the severe acute respiratory infection
             (SARI) through surveillance COVID-19; and
           two (2) cases of Humanitarian Aid Mission.

      Up to two (2) cases have been reported to be in intensive care unit
      and requires the help of a respirator but in stable condition.
      Of the two (2) case, one (1) case was being treated at Hospital Sungai
      Buloh, while one (1) case is being treated at the Sultanah Aminah Hospital,
      Johor Bharu.

      In addition, the Ministry of Health also conducted surveillance of COVID-19
      isolated or sporadic cases detected in Malaysia through the patient
      influenza-like-illness (ILI) and severe acute respiratory infection (SARI)
      that has nothing to do with travel history to countries
      or contact with affected COVID-19 positive cases. As of March 9
      2020, a total of 600 samples (138 and 462 SARI ILI) has been tested and
      so far one (1) the sample tested positive for infection COVID-19. Inquiry
      still being carried out to identify the source of infection cases
      This sporadic.

      Ministry of Health also announced today that all 66 Malaysians and
      family members brought home from Wuhan, China through Mission
      Humanitarian Aid on 26 February 2020, may be allowed to return
      to their homes after the end of the monitoring period
      Monitoring Center on Higher Education Leadership Academy (AKEPT).
      All of them are asymptomatic and repeat for all samples
      they have been tested negative.

      INFECTION SITUATION IN BRUNEI COVID-19

      International Health Regulations (IHR) Focal Point (FP) Brunei
      FP IHR informed Malaysia about confirmed cases COVID-
      19 detected in the country. The case was reported to have a history
      attending the sermons in the mosques Seri Petaling, Selangor
      February 27, 2020 until March 1, 2020.

      According to preliminary information, this involves gathering sermons
      the estimated number of 10,000 people from several countries, including
      Malaysia. Participants of Malaysia is estimated to exceed 5,000
      people. The State Health Department is investigating
      More on this subject in their respective states.

                    to cooperate with the Ministry of Health to ensure infection
                    COVID-19 does not continue to spread in the community.

i. all involving public assemblies (mass
                              gathering) to be postponed to prevent the possibility of infection
                              COVID 19th place;

                                advised not to attend the program which involves
                                public or crowded places, including religious activities;

                     iii. symptomatic individuals are advised to distance themselves (social
                                distancing) at least one meter when confronted
                                with other individuals;

                     iv. people not to travel to countries which are
                                experienced outbreaks COVID-19; and

                     v. Malaysians are experiencing symptoms tract infections
                                respiratory did not visit other areas in Malaysia
                                during the school holidays.
