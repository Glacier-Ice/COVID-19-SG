       Intensify surveillance ACTIVITIES IN
       COMMUNITY
       Ministry of Health would like to inform that early action
       implementation of surveillance activities have been carried out in the sector
       public and private sectors to identify transmission activities COVID-19
       the local community in Malaysia. Surveillance activities
       These include:
         • Clinical Surveillance of Influenza Like Illness (ILI) and Severe
          Acute Respiratory Infection (SARI) in clinics and hospitals
          Ministry of Health;
         • Screening COVID-19 for medical personnel;
         • Sampling before emergency surgery in hospital
          Ministry of Health;
         • Sampling in private laboratories.
       ILI sampling was conducted in eight (8) locations Clinic
       Health Ministry of Health of all the countries known as the location
       sentinel (sentinel site). This sampling includes all patients
       come to health clinics with symptoms of fever (temperature over
       38 ° C) with a cough and sore throat, but without any
       the risk of exposure to COVID-19 is known. A total of five (5)
       samples will be taken from each sentinel site and delivered
       to a laboratory for tests for the detection of COVID-19 surveillance analysis
       this.
       Meanwhile, the SARI sampling also involves tests
       detection COVID-19 for all patients with lung infection
       (pneumonia) on all MOH hospitals in Malaysia.
       To date, a total of 34.113 samples have been taken from
       surveillance activities. Out of this total,
       clinical sample of 6,100 is the surveillance of ILI / SARI, in
       where 71 samples tested positive for COVID-19 (ie 1.16 percent):
         • Cases of ILI: 8 COVID-19 positive cases from 1,322 samples
         • Cases of SARI: 63 positive cases COVID 19 of 4,778 samples


           In the case of SARI impairment COVID-19 positive cases were significantly
           between epidemiological week 17 (25 April 2020), in which only
           three (3) samples of 2,168 samples confirmed positive cases
           SARI taken (of 0.14 percent) compared with
           Epidemiological Week 12 (28 March 2020), in which 33 samples
           tested positive for the total sample of 263 SARI cases (ie 11:15
           percent).
           MOH also monitor COVID-19 test results conducted in
           the private sector, where the test was sent to a private laboratory. Obtained
           There are 84 positive cases detected than 22.081 tests conducted
           in private laboratories (ie 0.38 per cent).
           This surveillance activity definitely act as a system
           early detection (early detection) and early warning systems (early
           warning) for transmission COVID-19 in Malaysia. MOH will
           continue to carry out surveillance activities COVID-19 in Malaysia despite
           after the BCP is terminated, where these activities will be further strengthened
           they are included in the private sector and involve various zones.

           RELATED HEALTH ADVICE COVID-19
           Malaysians are advised to continue practicing stage
           optimal hygiene at all times, such as frequent washing
           hands with soap and water and apply
           safe social distance (social distancing) of at least 1 meter
           of others.
           Ministry of Health will continue to monitor the development of events COVID-19
           through all the information available and will be informed
           the people from time to time.
